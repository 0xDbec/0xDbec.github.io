02 / Selected work

Four engagements, in more depth.

01
GTM L2 to L2 Partner ops Ecosystem

Brainbot's cross-rollup bridging protocol, from early product through scaled GTM.

Beamer Bridge is Brainbot's L2 to L2 bridging protocol, built on an on-chain request and fulfillment model. I joined in 2021 as part of the founding product team and stayed three years as Growth Lead, building out a five-person growth and marketing team.

The scope covered the full go-to-market surface. That included ICP and positioning work with the product lead and art director, the weekly growth and product cadence where launch priorities were set, funnel instrumentation built with BI, and the content, community, and social strategy for the team.

Partner work with Arbitrum, Optimism, LiFi, Boba, Polygon zkEVM, and Gelato was coordinated jointly with engineering and operations rather than run downstream. The self-serve liquidity provider onboarding flow we shipped turned a manual, high-touch process into something repeatable, and the GTM blueprints from this period were reused on subsequent chain launches.

02
Positioning L1 Brand Ops

An L1 EVM for the creator and lifestyle space, in its formative phase.

LUKSO is an L1 EVM blockchain, originally designed for the fashion and lifestyle industry, pioneering early use cases around phygitals, 3D metaverse-ready assets, and NFT-enabled digital identities. I led marketing and business development during its formative phase.

The work included value proposition refinement, partnerships with fashion houses and Web3 projects, community and support operations, and the KPI tracking framework the team used day to day, set up with GA among other tools. Over time LUKSO has evolved into the broader creator and culture-focused chain it is today.

03
Activation Onboarding Developers

Off-chain payment channels on Ethereum, where growth meant fixing onboarding.

Raiden is Brainbot's off-chain payment channel technology on Ethereum, conceptually similar to Bitcoin's Lightning Network. I worked on it as Growth Lead. The fundamentals were strong, but developer activation had friction. Adoption was being gated by onboarding rather than reach.

I ran a targeted beta testing program to surface where developers were dropping out, then coordinated a cross-functional response with engineering, content, and support around a redesigned onboarding flow. Documentation and tutorials converted a lot of high-touch help into self-serve resources.

Results
+30%technical community growth in one month
